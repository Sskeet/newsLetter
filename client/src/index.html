Swift – Apple Developer:


Swift is a powerful and intuitive programming language for macOS, iOS, watchOS and tvOS.

Features of Swift
  • Faster, easier to use Strings that retain Unicode correctness and add support for creating, using and managing substrings.
  • Extends support of archival and serialization to struct and enum types and enables type-safety for serializing to external formats such as JSON and plist.
  • Enforced exclusive access to memory.
  • Much like Swift Playgrounds for iPad, playgrounds in Xcode make writing Swift code incredibly simple and fun. Type a line of code and the result appears immediately.

    • Advantage of Swift :
  • Open source: Swift creators acknowledged the fact that to build a defining programming language, the technology needs to be open for all. So, within its three years of existence, Swift acquired a large supportive community and an abundance of third-party tools.
  • Safe: Its syntax encourages you to write clean and consistent code which may even feel strict at times. Swift provides safeguards to prevent errors and improve readability.
  • Fast: Swift was built with performance in mind. Not only does its simple syntax and hand-holding help you develop faster, it also lives up to its name: as stated on apple.com, Swift is 2.6x faster than Objective-C and 8.4x faster than Python.
  • In demand: As of March 2018, it’s 12th most popular language, surpassing Objective-C, Go, Scala, and R. With more than 40K stars on GitHub and 187K StackOverflow questions, this young language is rightfully becoming one of the dominant technologies in the industry.

    Deal with Stressful Meetings
    1. Be Prepared : Make sure you have everything you need for your meeting prepared in advance. Advance preparation helps you feel organized and calm, which means decreased stress levels.

    2. Take a Time-Out :Soft music or relaxation and deep breathing techniques can help you release anxiety.Set aside five minutes before the meeting to collect your thoughts and calm your nerves.

    3. Acknowledge Your Triggers : Everyone has stressors, and acknowledging what gets you agitated can help you confront and alleviate your stress.

    4. Address Your Fears : If you have a fear of speaking in public, talk to your boss about it and find out if you can participate in the meeting in a less high-profile way. If that isn’t possible, consider getting involved with a public speaking group, such as Toastmasters, which can help you conquer these fears.

    5. Avoid Caffeine : Coffee is provided in nearly every business meeting, but the caffeine may make you jittery, anxious and stressed. Opt for decaf or, better yet, bring along a cold bottle of water.
